Welcome


I am currently a second year doctoral student in the department of Chemical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Professor Chrysanthos Gounaris. Our lab specializes in mathematical optimization modeling and algorithm development. I am most interested in research at the interface of chemical engineering, modeling, and computer science. My current projects involve the mathematical modeling and evaluation of materials design problems to identify optimal carbon-dioxide adsorbants.

I am also very committed to STEM outreach and teaching, and will be TA-ing an undergraduate course this Fall. For more information regarding my teaching work, see below.

My interests don't stop there! I am an artist, and have a partial portfolio of my paintings available to view here on my site. I also enjoy bike riding, hiking, climbing, and the great city of Pittsburgh!

Propel EAST Coding Club

(2015-2016)

For two consecutive years, my colleague Connor Brem and I developed and taught "Coding Club" at the Propel EAST School in Pittsburgh. This was an after school program for an hour each week in which middle school students got to learn introductory programming in a hands-on way.
